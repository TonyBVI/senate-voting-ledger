# Vision

## Long-Term Goal

Create the definitive public ledger of congressional voting records.

The Senate Voting Ledger exists to make voting records accessible, searchable,
auditable, and understandable while preserving the integrity of the official record.

## Mission

A neutral, auditable public ledger of Senate votes.

We do not tell users what to think.

We tell users how their representatives voted.

## Strategic Objectives

1. Preserve official voting records.
2. Make voting records easy to search and compare.
3. Enable citizen oversight of elected officials.
4. Provide reproducible analytics.
5. Maintain political neutrality.
6. Support public export of data.

## Roadmap

### Phase 1
- Senate vote ingestion
- Vote matrix
- Senator pages
- Vote detail pages

### Phase 2
- Filters
- Similarity analysis
- Attendance reporting
- Cross-party vote analysis

### Phase 3
- House integration
- Historical Congresses
- Public API
- Download center

### Phase 4
- AI-assisted querying
- Civic research tools
- Public dashboards

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- Raw records are immutable.
- Official wording is preserved.
- Every vote links to a source.
- Analytics must be reproducible.
- No partisan scoring system.
- No endorsements.

## Success Metric

A citizen should be able to answer:

"What has my representative actually voted for?"

in seconds rather than hours.
